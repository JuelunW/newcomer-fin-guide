Finland banking system

Finland has a very strict banking system. Account opening, fund transfers, and remittances are all heavily supervised. While in-person services are available, online and mobile banking are the primary and most convenient ways to manage your finances. Some of the most common banks in Finland are OP, Nordea, Danske Bank, S-Pankki, and Aktia.



Types of bank account?

In Finland, your daily banking account is generally referred to as a current account. There are various types of bank accounts, each serving different purposes. Different banks offer different types of accounts.

Service fees?

Yes. Almost every bank charges you daily banking service fee or monthly fee. However, there are exceptions. Asking your bank for more information or see below.

Interest?

Yes. But very low. Usually less than 0.5% per year.

How to open a bank account?

You should make an appointment before attending. If not, you would be asked to do that even though you walked-in a branch.

What do I need to bring?

Your identity documents: Passport, residence premit, ID card.
