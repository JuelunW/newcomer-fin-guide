Public transportation

This page introduces the public transportation system in Finland, including buses, trams, metros, trains, and city bikes. It also provides information on how to purchase tickets and where to find service points.

How are ticket prices calculated?

Ticket prices in Finland are generally calculated based on the distance traveled and the zones you pass through. Different cities and regions may have their own fare systems, so it's important to check the specific rules for the area you are in.

What are zones?

Take HSL as an example.

The HSL area is divided into four zones: A, B, C, and D. Zone A covers the city center of Helsinki, while zones B, C, and D cover the surrounding areas. The fare you pay depends on how many zones you travel through.

Different color means different routs

Take HSL as an example.

This color-coding is used on maps, at stops, and on the vehicles themselves to help passengers quickly identify the different types of transport and their routes.

City Bike

Take Helsinki as an example.

The bike system in Helsinki and Espoo is different from the system used in Vantaa, and bikes from the two different systems cannot be used interchangeably.

E-scooter

E-scooters are operated by private companies. You usually need to download their own app to use it. In general, the price is counted by the time you used.

VR

VR is the main train operator in Finland. It provides long-distance transport. You can check the schedule and buy tickets on their app, online, or ticket machines. Children, students, pensioners, conscripts and persons undergoing non-military service get a discount on train tickets. Groups with more than 18 persons can ask for a discount from our Group Sales.
