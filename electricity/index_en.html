Electricity Service

Voltage: 230 V
Frequency: 50 Hz
Plug type: type F and type C

The standard voltage and frequency in Finland is consistent with the European standard.

Electricity Contract

Electricity contract commonly refers to the contract for electricity consumption. You can choose an electricity provider based on your preference.

    There are two types contracts, you should be aware of.

  • Fixed-price contract

    All fixed-price contract go with fixed-term. It means you cannot change your decision during a contract. The typical term is one year, with pricing decreasing as the term length increases.

  • Spot price contract

    A continuous electricity contract that is tied to the market price with a margin. You can cancel this contract at anytime.

    • * Both consist of a basic service fee beside energy consumption fee.



The line chart of electricity spot price

Electricity network contract

It is also called distribution contract. You do not need to choose a network provider, because it is fixed based on your location. When you sign a consumption contract, you will make a network contract automatically. The network provider is responsible for maintaining the power lines and delivering electricity to your home.

How they charge you

Similar with consumption contract, the network bill is usually composed of a basic fee and an energy fee. The basic fee is a fixed monthly charge, while the energy fee is based on your electricity consumption (kWh). Some network providers may also charge additional fees for services such as meter reading or maintenance.
